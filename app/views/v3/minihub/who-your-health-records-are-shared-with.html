Health and care staff

Your full health records will only be seen by the health and care staff who are involved in your care.

Organisations

Some of the information from your health records can be used by organisations for different reasons:

  • NHS providers and commissioners - to plan where the NHS needs to improve or provide more health services
  • Pharmacies – to make sure they have enough stock of medicine by looking at what illnesses people have in their area
  • University researchers - to find cures and better treatments for illnesses
  • Charities – to look at NHS services and find ways to improve care
  • Software providers – to provide computer systems for the NHS to use and develop smartphone apps to help people manage illnesses
  • Pharmaceutical companies – to develop new medicines
  • Analytics services – to help the NHS analyse large amounts of health data

What organisations cannot do with your health records

Marketing and insurance

Your health records cannot be used by the NHS or other organisations for:

  • marketing purposes
  • insurance purposes

Your health records can only be used for marketing and insurance purposes if you ask for them to be used in this way.

Sharing your health records with third parties

Organisations have to sign legal contracts before they can use your health records.

The legal contract will state that health records cannot be given to any third parties, unless an independent review committee has approved it.

Choosing to opt out

You can choose to opt out of your confidential patient information being used for reasons other than your own individual care.
