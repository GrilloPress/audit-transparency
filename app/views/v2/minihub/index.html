The different types of health records

You have more than one health record.

The different types of health records include:

  • GP record
  • Hospital record
  • Dental record
  • Optician’s record
  • Sexual health record
  • Mental health record
  • Summary Care Record (short summary of your GP record)
Information:

You may not have all of these health records. For example, you will only have a sexual health record if you have been to a sexual health or genitourinary medicine (GUM) clinic.

What’s in your health records

Your health records include your name, age, address and NHS number.

Depending on the type of health record, they also have information about you such as your:

  • health conditions
  • medicine
  • allergies and past reactions to medicine
  • test results
  • hospital admissions
  • lifestyle information for example whether you smoke

Where your health records are held

Your health records apart from your Summary Care Record are held at the place you went to for treatment. For example, your GP record is held on your GP surgery’s computer system.

Your Summary Care Record is held on a central NHS database.

A Summary Care Record is a short summary of your GP record. Health and care staff can look at your Summary Care Record when you’re away from your GP surgery, for example:

  • in an emergency
  • at a walk in centre
  • at a pharmacy
